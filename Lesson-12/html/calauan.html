About Calauan

Calauan is a small municipality in the province of Laguna. Some parts of the town is being used by resettlers from Manila. Though it is a small city, it is known for its Pineapple plantations, Tilapia Hatcheries, Rice Fields, Plant Nurseries, Resorts, and Restaurants.

History of Calauan

It is believed that Calauan was named after the word "Kalawang" or rust during the colonization of the Spaniards. This was when the water that seeped through in the construction of the Municipal church became brown and rusty.

Interesting Facts

The patron saint of Calauan is Isidore the Laborer, the patron of farmers. During May 15 each year, Calauan celebrates the Pineapple Festival. There is a huge pineapple monument that is located in the town center of Calauan, Laguna.
